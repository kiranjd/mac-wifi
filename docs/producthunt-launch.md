## What MacWiFi Does

`MacWiFi` is a native macOS menu bar app that tells people whether their internet is actually good enough right now and whether the problem is likely their Wi-Fi or their ISP.

Current shipped capabilities:

- Native macOS menu bar app
- Calls / streaming / browsing readiness
- Plain-English diagnosis instead of just raw stats
- Live download and upload graph
- Wi-Fi-side vs internet-side breakdown
- Advanced radio and DNS diagnostics when needed
- One-time purchase with license activation

## Recommended First Comment

Hey Product Hunt,

I built `MacWiFi` because I kept hitting the same annoying situation:

the connection looked fine on paper, but calls still sounded bad, streaming still felt shaky, and I still couldn’t tell whether to blame my Wi-Fi or my ISP.

So I made the app I wanted for myself.

It lives in the menu bar and starts with the practical question first:

`is this connection actually good enough right now?`

Then it goes one step further and tries to answer the second question that usually matters just as much:

`is the weak spot my local Wi-Fi, or the internet path beyond it?`

I tried pretty hard to keep it from feeling like a tool made only for network people. The goal was not “more stats.” The goal was a quicker answer when your internet starts acting suspicious.

Current version includes:

- call / streaming / browsing readiness
- plain-English diagnosis
- live download / upload graph
- Wi-Fi vs ISP breakdown
- deeper radio and DNS diagnostics when you want them

It’s a small paid Mac app because I wanted it to stay simple and sustainable, not turn into another account-and-dashboard product.

If you try it, the feedback I’d love most is:

- did the diagnosis feel directionally right?
- did it help you decide what to do next faster?
- where does it still feel too technical?
